QuickBite

Food Ordering App
Mobile UI
Menu & Product Detail
Cart / CTA Focus
Virtual Internship Task

QuickBite is a mobile food ordering concept. The assignment was part of a virtual internship: design an experience where users can browse categories, see menu items with pricing, open an item, change quantity, and add it to cart.

I focused on making the app feel modern and appetizing: bold food images, clean pricing hierarchy, and an obvious “Add to Cart” button that stays visually anchored at the bottom. Navigation uses a familiar bottom bar for Home / Cart / Favorites / Profile.

Mobile Screens

Menu & Categories
Hero food shot + search bar + category tabs (“All / Pizza / Burger / Dessert / …”). Each item card shows image, name, price, and a short description. Bottom nav makes it feel like a modern delivery app.
Item Detail & Add to Cart
The product page highlights the food photo, description, quantity stepper, delivery ETA, price total, and a bold “Add To Cart” button. The layout is built to convert quickly.

Why this project matters

This wasn’t just visual exploration — it was treated like a real mobile product task. The goal was to prove I can design a clear commerce flow, think in components (cards, steppers, nav bars), and present something that feels shippable.

It also shows range: not only dashboards and web apps, but also mobile-first, B2C food ordering with emphasis on imagery, appetite appeal, and instant CTA.
