Rekbit

E-commerce UX
Mobile App (iOS / Android)
Branding
University Project

Rekbit is a delivery marketplace concept: browse categories, view products, add to cart, sign in, and complete checkout. I designed the full experience across web and mobile, including interface, and visual identity.

The goal: make ordering fast and familiar. Product cards are simple. Cart and checkout are clean. Branding is consistent across iOS, Android, and desktop so the user always feels like they’re still in the same product.

Web Experience

Homepage with hero banner, main categories, and flash deals.
Category / product list with pricing and “add to cart.”
Product detail page: description, quantity, related products.
Sign In screen. Clean, minimal, with social login options.
Sign Up screen mirrors Sign In for consistency.
Cart summary with editable quantities and a clear total.
Checkout: shipping address, payment method, and final order review are split into clear sections.

Mobile App (iOS / Android)

Sign In screen.
Sign Up screen.
Welcome / onboarding.
Categories with icons and promos.
Product list with price, volume, and promo tags.
Cart view with clean totals and quick quantity edits.
Checkout step 1 — Shipping Address.
Checkout step 2 — Payment.
Checkout step 3 — Review & confirm.
Product detail with description, related items, and add-to-cart.

Why this project matters

Rekbit was built as a university project, but I approached it like a real product. Instead of just drawing screens, I treated it as an end-to-end service: branding, marketplace UX, cart behavior, and final checkout logic.

The result is a full ecosystem — not just pretty UI. There’s a consistent brand voice, a responsive web layout for desktop, and a mobile experience that respects actual usage (late-night, one-hand, fast checkout).
