LebaDon

Donations / Crowdfunding UX
Web Platform
End-to-End Flow
Branding
University Project

LebaDon is a donation platform inspired by GoFundMe: users can browse active fundraisers, read the story behind each cause, and contribute directly. You can also create your own fundraiser with a goal, images, and description.

I designed the full flow for web: onboarding, browsing campaigns, viewing a fundraiser, and creating a new campaign. The goal was to make giving feel trustworthy and creating a fundraiser feel simple, not technical.

Platform Screens (Web)

Sign In screen. Minimal fields, social login support, “remember me.” The tone is reassuring — not aggressive, not corporate.
Sign Up screen. Same visual language as Sign In for consistency. The goal is to get you into the platform quickly so you can either donate or start your own fundraiser.
Homepage. Featured fundraisers and categories are surfaced first so urgent causes don’t get buried. Cards show title, amount raised, and short description.
Fundraiser detail page. Longer story, images, progress toward goal, and a clear Donate CTA. The layout is built to make the fundraiser feel human and legitimate, not scammy.
Create a fundraiser. The form walks you through title, target amount, story, and cover image. The priority was approachability: “you don’t need to know marketing to ask for help.”

Why this project matters

LebaDon was also a university project, but I treated it like a live product: real money, real urgency, and sensitive situations. The main challenge was balancing emotion with clarity — it has to feel personal, but still feel safe to donate.

This project shows that I can design not just shopping flows (like Rekbit), but also trust-driven flows: onboarding, credibility, story, and conversion.
