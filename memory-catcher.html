Memory Catcher

Brand Identity
Logo Design
Stationery System
Photography Studio
University Project

Memory Catcher is a fictional photography studio. I created a full brand kit: logo, color choices, stationery, and contact materials. The direction was luxury / classic — dark green and gold, serif typography, and a camera mark with a shutter icon.

The idea: “you’re trusting us with your memories.” So the brand needed to feel elegant and professional, not cheap / generic. Everything had to sit together like a real studio: business card, envelope, and letterhead.

Primary Logo

The logo combines a minimal camera outline with a stylized shutter icon, paired with a serif wordmark in gold. The gold + deep green palette is meant to feel premium and timeless — like wedding photography, portrait work, keepsake moments.

“Memory Catcher Photography” lockup. Gold accents communicate value and craft, not volume/cheapness. This is positioned as a service you trust for important life events.

Stationery & Brand Applications

The brand is applied consistently across all touchpoints: business card for direct contact, envelope and letterhead for formal communication / delivery of photos or invoices. The angled color block (green + gold) becomes a recognizable visual signature.

Business Card — Front
Front of the card features the logo on white with the diagonal green/gold accent framing the brand. Feels high-end, not casual.
Business Card — Back
Back shows full contact info: phone, email, location, website. Icons match the dark green brand color and keep it elegant.
Envelope — Front
Clean white front with the Memory Catcher wordmark. The gold mark + serif type make it feel like a studio, not just a print shop.
Envelope — Back
The angled green and gold block wraps the back flap. This creates a recognizable visual identity when handing off prints or invoices.
Letterhead
Official letter layout with header branding, multi-paragraph body text, and the same angled footer style plus contact info.

Why this project matters

This was a university project, but I treated it like a real studio brand. I wasn’t just drawing a logo — I built the full system so it could live in print, client communication, and presentation material.

It shows I can do more than product UI. I can define tone, color, type, and apply it consistently across touchpoints. That’s important for any product team, because brand and product UI have to feel like the same company.
